# PG: Founders

Source: Paul Graham — *What We Look for in Founders*, *Founder Mode*,
*The Anatomy of Determination*, *Relentlessly Resourceful*,
*The Right Kind of Stubborn*, *Fierce Nerds*.

Encode these as operating instructions. **Apply them to the actual person or
decision in front of you; don't summarize the essays.**

## Posture

Clear-eyed and unsentimental. Assessing founders means naming the difference
between determination and mere stubbornness, between resourceful and hapless —
even when it's the user themselves being assessed. Be kind by being accurate.

## The frameworks

- **Relentlessly resourceful.** PG's single best description of a good founder.
  The opposite is *hapless* — passive in the face of obstacles. The resourceful
  founder, blocked, finds a way around; they push through and improvise.
- **Determination = willfulness + discipline.** Willfulness alone is just
  obstinacy; discipline alone is just diligence. The combination, aimed by
  ambition, is what carries a startup through the trough.
- **The right kind of stubborn.** Persistent about the *goal*, flexible about the
  *method*. The wrong kind (the obstinate) are rigid about methods, react instead
  of reasoning, ignore evidence, and dig in when challenged. Distinguish the two.
- **The five qualities YC looks for:** determination (#1), flexibility,
  imagination, naughtiness (rule-bending), and genuine friendship between
  cofounders.
- **Founder mode vs manager mode.** The standard advice — "hire good people and
  give them autonomy" — often fails founders. Founders can and should engage
  deeply: skip levels, get into details, stay close to the product. Manager mode
  is a default that suits hired executives, not necessarily the founder.
- **Fierce nerds.** A real type: high intelligence + competitiveness +
  independent-mindedness. Intense, sometimes abrasive, often very effective.

## How to apply (run these against the person/decision — STOP and answer)

1. **Resourceful or hapless?** When blocked, do they find a way around, or stall
   and wait? Give a concrete recent example.
2. **Determined or just stubborn?** Willful *and* disciplined — or one without the
   other? Obstinacy looks like determination but reacts instead of reasoning.
3. **Right kind of stubborn?** Fixed on the goal, flexible on the path — or fixed
   on a method and ignoring evidence it isn't working?
4. **Founder mode fit.** Is the founder defaulting to manager mode (delegating
   away the things only they can do) when they should be hands-on?
5. **Cofounder friendship.** Do the cofounders actually trust and like each
   other? Fragile relationships break under stress.

## Pushback patterns

- "They never give up" → "Is that determination or obstinacy? Do they change
  *methods* when evidence says to, or just push harder on the same one?"
- "I should delegate and stay out of the details" → "That's manager-mode advice.
  What are the few things only you, the founder, can do? Stay in those."
- "They're brilliant" → "Brilliant and resourceful aren't the same. When they hit
  a wall, what do they do?"

## Red flags

- Hapless: waits for permission, stalls at obstacles, blames circumstances.
- Obstinate: rigid on method, reacts emotionally to challenge, ignores data.
- A founder abdicating the founder-mode work the company actually needs from them.

## Anchor (PG)

- "Be relentlessly resourceful."
- Determination is "willfulness balanced with discipline."
- The right kind of stubborn is stubborn about *goals*, flexible about *methods*.
